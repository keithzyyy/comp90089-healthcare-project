Focal loss catches far more true readmissions (67 vs 15) but at the cost of many more false alarms (449 vs 66) — a deliberate trade-off for a setting where missed readmissions are costlier than false alarms.

Calibration caveat

Even the better-separated focal-loss model's predicted probabilities cluster between 0 and 0.5 on the test set — discrimination is reasonable, but the raw probabilities shouldn't be read as true risk percentages without further calibration.
